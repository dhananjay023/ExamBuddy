Chapter 7: Triangles

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Triangles are one of the most fundamental shapes in geometry. This chapter explores the properties of triangles, their classification, congruence criteria, and important theorems related to triangles. Understanding triangles is essential for solving complex geometric problems and real-world applications.

Key Concepts

Types of Triangles

  • Based on Angles:
    • Acute triangle: All angles less than 90°
    • Right triangle: One angle equals 90°
    • Obtuse triangle: One angle greater than 90°
  • Based on Sides:
    • Scalene triangle: All sides different
    • Isosceles triangle: Two sides equal
    • Equilateral triangle: All sides equal

Congruence of Triangles

  • SSS (Side-Side-Side) criterion
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side) criterion
  • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle) criterion
  • RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side) criterion
  • Properties of congruent triangles:
    • Corresponding sides are equal
    • Corresponding angles are equal

Properties of Triangles

  • Sum of angles = 180°
  • Exterior angle = Sum of opposite interior angles
  • Triangle inequality theorem
  • Properties of isosceles triangle:
    • Equal sides have equal angles opposite to them
    • Altitude to base bisects it
  • Properties of equilateral triangle:
    • All sides equal
    • All angles equal to 60°
    • All medians equal

Important Lines and Points

  • Median: Line from vertex to midpoint of opposite side
  • Altitude: Perpendicular from vertex to opposite side
  • Angle bisector: Divides angle into two equal parts
  • Centroid: Intersection of medians
  • Orthocenter: Intersection of altitudes
  • Circumcenter: Intersection of perpendicular bisectors
  • Incenter: Intersection of angle bisectors

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Prove that angles opposite to equal sides of an isosceles triangle are equal.

Solution:

  1. Given: Triangle ABC with AB = AC
  2. To prove: ∠B = ∠C
  3. Proof:
    • Consider triangle ABC and its mirror image
    • By SAS congruence:
      • AB = AC (given)
      • ∠A is common
      • AB = AC (given)
    • Therefore, triangles are congruent
    • Hence, ∠B = ∠C

Question 2

In triangle ABC, if D is the midpoint of BC and AD is drawn, prove that area of triangle ABD = area of triangle ACD.

Solution:

  1. Given: D is midpoint of BC
  2. To prove: Area of triangle ABD = Area of triangle ACD
  3. Proof:
    • Area of triangle = ½ × base × height
    • Both triangles ABD and ACD have:
      • Same height (perpendicular distance from A to BC)
      • Equal bases (BD = DC as D is midpoint)
    • Therefore, areas are equal

Key Formulas

Angle Sum

∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

Area

A = ½ × base × height

Exterior Angle

∠ext = ∠A + ∠B

Pythagoras Theorem

a² + b² = c² (in right triangle)

Common Misconceptions

Triangle Inequality

Misconception: Any three lengths can form a triangle.

Correction: Sum of any two sides must be greater than the third side.

Congruence vs Similarity

Misconception: Congruent triangles are the same as similar triangles.

Correction: Congruent triangles are identical in size and shape, while similar triangles have same shape but may differ in size.

Isosceles Triangle

Misconception: An isosceles triangle has exactly two equal sides.

Correction: An isosceles triangle has at least two equal sides - equilateral triangles are also isosceles.
