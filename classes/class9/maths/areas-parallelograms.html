Chapter 9: Areas of Parallelograms and Triangles

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter focuses on understanding and calculating the areas of parallelograms and triangles. We'll explore various methods and theorems related to areas, including the relationships between figures on the same base and between the same parallel lines.

Key Concepts

Area of Parallelogram

  • Area = base × height (perpendicular height)
  • Height must be perpendicular to the base
  • Area is independent of the slant of the parallelogram
  • Parallelograms on same base and between same parallel lines have equal areas
  • Different shaped parallelograms can have same area

Area of Triangle

  • Area = ½ × base × height
  • Area = ½ × base × perpendicular height
  • Triangles on same base and between same parallel lines have equal areas
  • Area can be calculated using different formulas:
    • Basic formula: ½bh
    • Heron's formula (when sides are known)
    • Trigonometric formula: ½ab sin C

Important Theorems

  • Figures on the same base and between the same parallel lines have equal areas
  • A diagonal of a parallelogram divides it into two triangles of equal area
  • The median of a triangle divides it into two triangles of equal area
  • Area of triangle = ½ × area of parallelogram (when on same base and height)
  • Area ratio theorem for line dividing two sides of triangle

Applications

  • Finding areas of irregular polygons
  • Land surveying and measurement
  • Architectural design and planning
  • Comparing areas of different shapes
  • Problem-solving in real-world scenarios

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Prove that a median of a triangle divides it into two triangles of equal area.

Solution:

  1. Given: Triangle ABC with median AD to side BC
  2. To prove: Area of ABD = Area of ACD
  3. Proof:
    • Area of triangle = ½ × base × height
    • Both triangles ABD and ACD have:
      • Same height (perpendicular distance from A to BC)
      • Equal bases (BD = DC as D is midpoint)
    • Therefore, Area of ABD = Area of ACD

Question 2

Show that parallelograms on the same base and between the same parallel lines are equal in area.

Solution:

  1. Given: Two parallelograms ABCD and ABEF on same base AB and between parallel lines AB and CF
  2. To prove: Area of ABCD = Area of ABEF
  3. Proof:
    • Area of parallelogram = base × height
    • Both parallelograms have:
      • Same base (AB)
      • Same height (perpendicular distance between parallel lines)
    • Therefore, Area of ABCD = Area of ABEF

Key Formulas

Area of Parallelogram

A = base × height

Area of Triangle

A = ½ × base × height

Area Ratio

A₁:A₂ = b₁:b₂

Area by Coordinates

A = ½|x₁(y₂-y₃) + x₂(y₃-y₁) + x₃(y₁-y₂)|

Common Misconceptions

Height Measurement

Misconception: Height is always the vertical distance.

Correction: Height must be measured perpendicular to the base, regardless of orientation.

Base Selection

Misconception: Only the bottom side can be the base.

Correction: Any side can be chosen as the base, with height measured perpendicular to it.

Area Comparison

Misconception: Different shaped figures always have different areas.

Correction: Figures with different shapes can have equal areas if they share the same base and height between parallel lines.
