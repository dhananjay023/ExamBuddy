Chapter 12: Heron's Formula

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Heron's Formula provides a way to calculate the area of a triangle when all three sides are known, without needing to know the height or angles. Named after Hero of Alexandria, this formula is particularly useful when direct measurement of height is difficult or impossible.

Key Concepts

Understanding Heron's Formula

  • Semi-perimeter (s):
    • s = (a + b + c)/2
    • where a, b, c are sides of triangle
  • Area Formula:
    • Area = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c))
    • Always gives positive value
    • Works for all types of triangles
  • Conditions:
    • Triangle inequality must be satisfied
    • All sides must be positive
    • Sum of any two sides > third side

Applications

  • Finding areas of:
    • Irregular triangular plots
    • Triangular architectural elements
    • Triangular structural components
  • Real-world uses:
    • Land surveying
    • Architecture
    • Engineering
    • Construction

Related Concepts

  • Area of triangular regions
  • Perimeter calculations
  • Triangle inequality theorem
  • Types of triangles:
    • Acute triangles
    • Right triangles
    • Obtuse triangles

Problem-Solving Steps

  • Verify triangle inequality
  • Calculate semi-perimeter
  • Apply Heron's formula
  • Simplify the expression
  • Common errors to avoid:
    • Using incorrect units
    • Forgetting to simplify under root
    • Not verifying triangle inequality

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Find the area of a triangle with sides 8 cm, 15 cm, and 17 cm.

Solution:

  1. Given: a = 8 cm, b = 15 cm, c = 17 cm
  2. Step 1: Calculate semi-perimeter (s)
    • s = (a + b + c)/2
    • s = (8 + 15 + 17)/2 = 40/2 = 20 cm
  3. Step 2: Apply Heron's formula
    • Area = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c))
    • = √(20(20-8)(20-15)(20-17))
    • = √(20 × 12 × 5 × 3)
    • = √3600
    • = 60 square cm

Question 2

A triangular park has sides 120 m, 80 m, and 100 m. Find its area using Heron's formula.

Solution:

  1. Given: a = 120 m, b = 80 m, c = 100 m
  2. Step 1: Calculate semi-perimeter (s)
    • s = (120 + 80 + 100)/2
    • s = 300/2 = 150 m
  3. Step 2: Apply Heron's formula
    • Area = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c))
    • = √(150(150-120)(150-80)(150-100))
    • = √(150 × 30 × 70 × 50)
    • = √15,750,000
    • = 3,967.18 square meters

Key Formulas

Semi-perimeter

s = (a + b + c)/2

Heron's Formula

A = √(s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c))

Triangle Inequality

a + b > c, b + c > a, c + a > b

Perimeter

P = a + b + c

Common Misconceptions

Formula Application

Misconception: Heron's formula works for any three numbers.

Correction: The three sides must satisfy triangle inequality theorem to form a valid triangle.

Semi-perimeter

Misconception: Semi-perimeter is half of any two sides.

Correction: Semi-perimeter is half of the sum of all three sides.

Units

Misconception: Units remain same after area calculation.

Correction: Area units are square units (e.g., cm² if sides are in cm).
