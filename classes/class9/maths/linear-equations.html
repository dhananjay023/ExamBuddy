Chapter 4: Linear Equations in Two Variables

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Linear equations in two variables are algebraic equations where the highest power of variables is 1. This chapter extends your understanding of linear equations from one variable to two variables, introducing concepts of graphing and solving systems of equations.

Key Concepts

Linear Equation

  • General form: ax + by + c = 0, where a and b are not both zero
  • x and y are variables
  • a, b are coefficients
  • c is the constant term
  • Examples:
    • 2x + 3y = 6
    • x - y = 4
    • 3x + 2y + 5 = 0

Solutions of Linear Equations

  • A solution is an ordered pair (x,y) that satisfies the equation
  • Infinite number of solutions exist
  • Solutions form a straight line when plotted
  • Methods to find solutions:
    • Graphical method
    • Substitution method
    • Trial and error

Graphing Linear Equations

  • Plot points by finding ordered pairs
  • Two points are sufficient to draw a line
  • x-intercept: point where line crosses x-axis (y=0)
  • y-intercept: point where line crosses y-axis (x=0)
  • Slope: measures steepness of the line

System of Linear Equations

  • Two equations in two variables
  • Solution is the point of intersection
  • Types of solutions:
    • Unique solution (lines intersect)
    • No solution (parallel lines)
    • Infinitely many solutions (coincident lines)

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Find the value of k for which the system of equations 2x + 3y = 7 and kx + 4y = 8 has a unique solution.

Solution:

For a unique solution, the lines must intersect. Therefore:

  1. The ratio of coefficients must not be equal
  2. 2/k ≠ 3/4
  3. 8 ≠ 3k
  4. k ≠ 8/3

Therefore, k can be any value except 8/3.

Question 2

Draw the graph of 2x + y = 6 and find its intercepts.

Solution:

  1. For x-intercept, put y = 0:
    • 2x + 0 = 6
    • x = 3
    • x-intercept is (3,0)
  2. For y-intercept, put x = 0:
    • 2(0) + y = 6
    • y = 6
    • y-intercept is (0,6)
  3. Plot these points and join them to get the line

Key Formulas

General Form

ax + by + c = 0

Slope Formula

m = (y₂-y₁)/(x₂-x₁)

Point-Slope Form

y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)

Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + c

Common Misconceptions

Vertical Lines

Misconception: All lines have a slope.

Correction: Vertical lines have undefined slope as division by zero is undefined.

Number of Solutions

Misconception: Every system of equations has a solution.

Correction: Parallel lines have no solution as they never intersect.

Graphing Points

Misconception: Only integer points can be plotted.

Correction: Any real number coordinates can be plotted on the graph.
