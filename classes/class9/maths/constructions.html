Chapter 11: Constructions

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Geometric constructions are fundamental to understanding geometry. This chapter covers basic and advanced constructions using compass and ruler, including triangles, perpendicular lines, angle bisectors, and more. These constructions form the basis for more complex geometric problems and real-world applications.

Key Concepts

Basic Construction Tools

  • Compass: For drawing arcs and circles
  • Ruler: For drawing straight lines
  • Protractor: For measuring angles (not used in constructions)
  • Basic operations:
    • Drawing perpendicular lines
    • Bisecting angles
    • Bisecting line segments
    • Copying angles

Triangle Construction

  • Given three sides (SSS)
  • Given two sides and included angle (SAS)
  • Given two angles and included side (ASA)
  • Given hypotenuse and one side (RHS)
  • Steps for each construction:
    • Draw base line
    • Use compass to mark required lengths
    • Draw arcs to locate third vertex
    • Join points to complete triangle

Special Constructions

  • Perpendicular bisector of a line segment
  • Angle bisector
  • Perpendicular line from a point to a line
  • Parallel lines
  • Special angles:
    • 60 degrees
    • 30 degrees
    • 45 degrees
    • 90 degrees

Applications

  • Architectural drawing
  • Engineering design
  • Art and design
  • Problem-solving in geometry
  • Real-world applications:
    • Building construction
    • Surveying
    • Navigation
    • Technical drawing

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Construct a triangle ABC in which BC = 6 cm, AB = 5 cm, and ∠ABC = 60°.

Solution:

  1. Steps of construction:
    • Draw line segment BC = 6 cm
    • At point B, construct angle of 60° using compass
    • Cut an arc of radius 5 cm from B to intersect the ray at A
    • Join AC to complete the triangle
  2. Verification:
    • Measure BC = 6 cm
    • Measure AB = 5 cm
    • Measure ∠ABC = 60°

Question 2

Construct the perpendicular bisector of a line segment AB of length 7.6 cm.

Solution:

  1. Steps of construction:
    • Draw line segment AB = 7.6 cm
    • With center A, draw an arc of radius more than half of AB
    • With same radius and center B, draw another arc intersecting the first arc at P and Q
    • Join PQ to get perpendicular bisector
  2. Verification:
    • PQ ⊥ AB
    • PQ bisects AB at point M
    • AM = MB

Key Formulas

Common Misconceptions

Construction Accuracy

Misconception: Constructions don't need to be precise.

Correction: Accuracy in construction is crucial for correct geometric relationships.

Tools Usage

Misconception: Protractor can be used for constructions.

Correction: Only compass and ruler are allowed in geometric constructions.

Triangle Construction

Misconception: Any three measurements can construct a triangle.

Correction: Certain combinations of measurements may not form a valid triangle.
