Chapter 14: Statistics

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, analyzing, and interpreting numerical data. This chapter introduces fundamental concepts of statistics, including data collection methods, data representation, and measures of central tendency. Understanding statistics is crucial for making informed decisions based on data in various fields like economics, science, and social studies.

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Find the mean of the following data: 12, 14, 16, 18, 20, 22, 24.

Solution:

  1. Using direct method:
    • Mean = (Sum of observations)/(Number of observations)
    • = (12 + 14 + 16 + 18 + 20 + 22 + 24)/7
    • = 126/7
    • = 18
  2. Verification:
    • The mean (18) is the central value
    • There are 3 values below and 3 values above

Question 2

The following data shows the heights (in cm) of 50 students: Height: 150-154, 155-159, 160-164, 165-169, 170-174 Frequency: 8, 12, 15, 10, 5 Find the median height.

Solution:

  1. Step 1: Find N/2 (N = total frequency)
    • N = 8 + 12 + 15 + 10 + 5 = 50
    • N/2 = 25
  2. Step 2: Locate median class
    • Cumulative frequency: 8, 20, 35, 45, 50
    • Median class: 160-164 (where 25 lies)
  3. Step 3: Apply median formula
    • Median = l + h(N/2 - cf)/f
    • = 160 + 5(25 - 20)/15
    • = 160 + 5(5/15)
    • = 160 + 1.67
    • = 161.67 cm

Key Formulas

Mean (Arithmetic)

x̄ = Σx/n (ungrouped)
x̄ = Σfx/Σf (grouped)

Median (Grouped)

Median = l + ((n/2 - cf)/f) × h
where l = lower limit
h = class size

Mode (Grouped)

Mode = l + ((f₁ - f₀)/(2f₁ - f₀ - f₂)) × h

Class Size

h = Upper limit - Lower limit

Common Misconceptions

Mean vs Median vs Mode

Misconception: Mean is always the best measure of central tendency.

Correction: Each measure has its use case - mean for normal distributions, median for skewed data, mode for categorical data.

Class Intervals

Misconception: Class intervals must always be equal.

Correction: Unequal class intervals can be used with frequency density for better representation.

Sample vs Population

Misconception: Statistics from a sample exactly represent the population.

Correction: Sample statistics are estimates with some margin of error.
