Chapter 10: Circles

Class 9 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

A circle is a collection of points equidistant from a fixed point called the center. This chapter explores the properties of circles, including angles, chords, arcs, and their relationships. Understanding circles is fundamental to geometry and has numerous real-world applications.

Key Concepts

Basic Terms

  • Center: Fixed point from which all points on circle are equidistant
  • Radius: Line segment from center to any point on circle
  • Diameter: Line segment through center connecting two points on circle
  • Chord: Line segment connecting any two points on circle
  • Arc: Part of circle between two points
  • Sector: Region between two radii and arc
  • Segment: Region between chord and arc

Circle Properties

  • Equal radii: All radii of a circle are equal
  • Diameter = 2 × radius
  • Perpendicular from center to chord bisects the chord
  • Equal chords are equidistant from center
  • Equal arcs have equal central angles
  • Angles in same segment are equal
  • Angle in semicircle is right angle

Theorems

  • Equal chords subtend equal angles at center
  • Perpendicular from center to chord bisects it
  • Line through center perpendicular to chord bisects it
  • Angles in same segment are equal
  • Cyclic quadrilateral properties
  • Tangent-radius relationship

Circle Measurements

  • Circumference = 2πr
  • Area = πr²
  • Length of arc = (θ/360°) × 2πr
  • Area of sector = (θ/360°) × πr²
  • Area of segment = Area of sector - Area of triangle

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Prove that equal chords of a circle are equidistant from the center.

Solution:

  1. Given: Circle with center O and equal chords AB and CD
  2. To prove: Perpendicular distances from O to AB and CD are equal
  3. Proof:
    • Draw perpendiculars OM and ON to chords AB and CD
    • Join OA and OC
    • In triangles OMA and ONC:
      • OA = OC (radii)
      • AB = CD (given)
      • AM = AB/2 and CN = CD/2 (perpendicular from center bisects chord)
      • ∠OMA = ∠ONC = 90° (given)
    • By RHS congruence, triangles are congruent
    • Therefore, OM = ON

Question 2

If two circles intersect at two points, prove that their centers are equidistant from the line joining the points of intersection.

Solution:

  1. Given: Two circles with centers O and O' intersecting at A and B
  2. To prove: Perpendicular distances from O and O' to AB are equal
  3. Proof:
    • AB is common chord
    • Line joining centers is perpendicular bisector of common chord
    • Therefore, O and O' are equidistant from AB

Key Formulas

Circumference

C = 2πr

Area

A = πr²

Arc Length

L = (θ/360°) × 2πr

Sector Area

A = (θ/360°) × πr²

Common Misconceptions

Diameter and Radius

Misconception: Any line through a circle is a diameter.

Correction: A diameter must pass through the center of the circle.

Arc and Chord

Misconception: Arc length is equal to its chord length.

Correction: Arc length is always greater than its chord length (except for very small arcs).

Circle Properties

Misconception: All lines that bisect a chord pass through the center.

Correction: Only the perpendicular bisector of a chord passes through the center.
