Chapter 1: Matter in Our Surroundings

Class 9 Science Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Matter in Our Surroundings is a fundamental chapter that introduces students to the basic concepts of matter, its properties, and states. This chapter helps understand how matter exists in different forms around us and the factors that affect its state.

Key Concepts

Matter and its Nature

  • Matter is anything that occupies space and has mass
  • Matter is made up of particles
  • Particles of matter have space between them
  • Particles of matter are continuously moving
  • Particles of matter attract each other

States of Matter

  • Solid state - fixed shape and volume
  • Liquid state - fixed volume but takes shape of container
  • Gaseous state - neither fixed shape nor volume
  • Plasma and Bose-Einstein condensate are other states
  • States can be interconverted by changing temperature and pressure

Change of States

  • Melting: Solid to Liquid
  • Freezing: Liquid to Solid
  • Vaporization: Liquid to Gas
  • Condensation: Gas to Liquid
  • Sublimation: Solid directly to Gas

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Why does a gas fill the container completely while a solid and liquid do not?

Solution:

This happens because:

  1. In gases, the particles have very weak forces of attraction between them
  2. Gas particles have high kinetic energy and move randomly in all directions
  3. The particles in gases are far apart from each other
  4. Due to these properties, gas particles can move freely and spread out to fill the entire container

Question 2

Convert 373K to °C and °F.

Solution:

To convert Kelvin to Celsius:

°C = K - 273

373K - 273 = 100°C

To convert Celsius to Fahrenheit:

°F = (°C × 9/5) + 32

(100 × 9/5) + 32 = 212°F

Therefore, 373K = 100°C = 212°F

Common Misconceptions

Temperature and Heat

Misconception: Temperature and heat are the same thing.

Correction: Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of particles, while heat is the transfer of thermal energy between objects.

Boiling and Evaporation

Misconception: Boiling and evaporation are the same process.

Correction: Evaporation occurs at any temperature, while boiling occurs only at the boiling point and requires the entire liquid mass.

Matter in Space

Misconception: There is no matter in space.

Correction: Space contains matter in various forms, including gases, dust particles, and plasma.
