Chapter 3: Atoms and Molecules

Class 9 Science Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter delves into the fundamental building blocks of matter - atoms and molecules. It explores how atoms combine to form molecules, the laws governing their combinations, and how we represent them using chemical formulae.

Key Concepts

Laws of Chemical Combination

  • Law of Conservation of Mass
  • Law of Constant Proportions
  • Law of Multiple Proportions
  • Gay Lussac's Law of Gaseous Volumes
  • Avogadro's Law

Atoms

  • Smallest particle of an element that can exist independently
  • Atoms of same element are identical
  • Atoms can neither be created nor destroyed
  • Atoms combine in whole number ratios
  • Atomic mass unit (amu) is the unit of measurement

Molecules

  • Smallest particle of matter that can exist independently
  • Can be atoms or groups of atoms
  • Molecules of elements vs compounds
  • Molecular mass calculations
  • Chemical formulae and their significance

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Calculate the molecular mass of H2SO4 (Atomic mass: H = 1, S = 32, O = 16)

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Mass of H atoms = 2 × 1 = 2 amu
  2. Mass of S atom = 1 × 32 = 32 amu
  3. Mass of O atoms = 4 × 16 = 64 amu
  4. Total molecular mass = 2 + 32 + 64 = 98 amu

Therefore, the molecular mass of H2SO4 is 98 amu

Question 2

State and explain the Law of Conservation of Mass with an example.

Solution:

The Law of Conservation of Mass states that mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. The total mass of reactants equals the total mass of products.

Example: When silver nitrate reacts with sodium chloride:

AgNO3 + NaCl → AgCl + NaNO3

  • Mass of AgNO3 = 170 g
  • Mass of NaCl = 58.5 g
  • Total mass of reactants = 228.5 g
  • Mass of AgCl = 143.5 g
  • Mass of NaNO3 = 85 g
  • Total mass of products = 228.5 g

The mass is conserved as total mass of reactants equals total mass of products.

Key Formulas

Molecular Mass

Molecular Mass = Sum of atomic masses of all atoms in the molecule

Mole Concept

1 mole = 6.022 × 10²³ particles

Molar Mass

Molar Mass (g/mol) = Mass of 1 mole of substance

Common Misconceptions

Atoms vs Molecules

Misconception: All elements exist as single atoms.

Correction: Many elements exist as diatomic molecules (H2, O2, N2, etc.) in their natural state.

Molecular Mass vs Atomic Mass

Misconception: Molecular mass and atomic mass are the same.

Correction: Molecular mass is the sum of atomic masses of all atoms in a molecule, while atomic mass is the mass of a single atom.

Chemical Formulas

Misconception: Subscripts in chemical formulas can be written in any order.

Correction: Chemical formulas follow specific conventions for writing elements in a particular order.
