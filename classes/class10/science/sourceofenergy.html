Chapter 14: Sources of Energy

Class 10 Science Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Sources of Energy is a crucial chapter that explores various energy resources available to us, their utilization, and environmental impact. This chapter helps students understand the importance of sustainable energy sources and the need for efficient energy management in the modern world.

Key Concepts

Classification of Energy Sources

  • Conventional Sources:
    • Fossil fuels (coal, petroleum, natural gas)
    • Thermal power plants
    • Hydroelectric power
  • Non-conventional Sources:
    • Solar energy
    • Wind energy
    • Biomass
    • Tidal energy
    • Geothermal energy
    • Nuclear energy

Fossil Fuels

  • Formed from decomposition of organic matter over millions of years
  • Non-renewable energy sources
  • Types:
    • Coal - Used in thermal power plants
    • Petroleum - Transportation and industrial use
    • Natural Gas - Domestic and industrial fuel
  • Environmental concerns:
    • Air pollution
    • Greenhouse gas emissions
    • Global warming

Solar Energy

  • Clean and renewable source of energy
  • Applications:
    • Solar cookers
    • Solar water heaters
    • Solar cells for electricity
    • Solar panels for power generation
  • Advantages:
    • No pollution
    • Renewable source
    • Low maintenance cost
  • Limitations:
    • Weather dependent
    • High initial cost
    • Storage issues

Wind Energy

  • Kinetic energy of wind converted to mechanical/electrical energy
  • Uses windmills and wind turbines
  • Applications:
    • Electricity generation
    • Water pumping
    • Grinding grain
  • Advantages:
    • Renewable source
    • No pollution
    • Low operating cost
  • Limitations:
    • Wind speed variation
    • Large land requirement
    • Initial setup cost

Hydroelectric Power

  • Uses potential energy of falling water
  • Components:
    • Dam for water storage
    • Turbines for power generation
    • Generators for electricity conversion
  • Advantages:
    • Clean energy source
    • Long operational life
    • Multiple uses of dam
  • Disadvantages:
    • Environmental impact
    • Displacement of people
    • High construction cost

Nuclear Energy

  • Energy released from nuclear fission/fusion
  • Process:
    • Nuclear fission of uranium
    • Heat generation
    • Steam production
    • Turbine operation
  • Advantages:
    • High energy output
    • Low fuel requirement
    • No air pollution
  • Concerns:
    • Nuclear waste disposal
    • Safety risks
    • High setup cost

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

What are the advantages and disadvantages of using solar energy? Explain the working principle of a solar cooker.

Solution:

Advantages of Solar Energy:

  • Renewable source of energy
  • No environmental pollution
  • Available in abundance
  • Low maintenance cost
  • Can be used in remote areas

Disadvantages:

  • High initial cost
  • Weather dependent
  • Energy storage is expensive
  • Requires large area for installation
  • Not available at night

Solar Cooker Working Principle:

  1. Solar Radiation: Sun's rays enter through glass cover
  2. Greenhouse Effect: Glass traps heat inside box
  3. Absorption: Black surface absorbs heat
  4. Reflection: Mirrors reflect additional sunlight
  5. Heat Retention: Insulation prevents heat loss
  6. Cooking: Food gets cooked by trapped heat

Question 2

Compare and contrast conventional and non-conventional sources of energy.

Solution:

Aspect Conventional Sources Non-conventional Sources
Availability Limited, depleting Unlimited, renewable
Environmental Impact High pollution Minimal pollution
Cost Lower initial cost Higher initial cost
Technology Well established Developing
Examples Coal, petroleum, natural gas Solar, wind, biomass

Question 3

Explain the working of a hydroelectric power plant. What are its advantages and limitations?

Solution:

Working of Hydroelectric Power Plant:

  1. Water Storage: Dam stores water at height
  2. Potential Energy: Water gains potential energy due to height
  3. Kinetic Energy: Falling water converts potential to kinetic energy
  4. Mechanical Energy: Water rotates turbines
  5. Electrical Energy: Generator converts mechanical to electrical energy

Advantages:

  • Renewable source of energy
  • No fuel cost
  • No air pollution
  • Long operational life
  • Additional benefits like irrigation

Limitations:

  • High construction cost
  • Large land requirement
  • Environmental impact
  • Displacement of local population
  • Dependent on rainfall

Key Points to Remember

Good Source of Energy Should Have

- High calorific value
- Easy availability
- Low cost
- Easy storage and transport
- Environment friendly

Energy Conservation Tips

- Use energy-efficient appliances
- Switch off when not in use
- Use natural light when possible
- Regular maintenance of appliances

Renewable vs Non-renewable

Renewable: Replenished naturally
Non-renewable: Limited, depleting

Common Misconceptions

Renewable Energy Cost

Misconception: Renewable energy is always more expensive than conventional sources.

Correction: While initial setup costs may be higher, long-term operational costs are often lower for renewable sources.

Nuclear Energy

Misconception: Nuclear power plants always cause radiation hazards.

Correction: Modern nuclear plants have multiple safety measures and normal operation doesn't release harmful radiation.

Solar Energy Limitations

Misconception: Solar energy is only useful in hot climates.

Correction: Solar panels work on daylight, not heat, and can be effective even in cold climates with adequate sunlight.
