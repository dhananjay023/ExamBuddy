Chapter 11: The Human Eye and Colorful World

Class 10 Science Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

The Human Eye and Colorful World is a fascinating chapter that explores the structure and functioning of the human eye, various defects of vision, and the phenomena that create the colorful world we see around us. This chapter combines biology and physics to explain how we perceive light and color.

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

What is the far point and near point of the human eye? How do these points change with age?

Solution:

Far Point:

  • The maximum distance at which an object can be seen clearly
  • For a normal eye, it is at infinity
  • In myopia, far point comes closer than infinity

Near Point:

  • The minimum distance at which an object can be seen clearly
  • For a normal eye, it is about 25 cm
  • With age, the near point gradually recedes (moves away)

Changes with Age:

  • Young children: Near point can be as close as 7-8 cm
  • Adults (around 40 years): Near point starts moving away from 25 cm
  • Old age: Near point may move to 1-2 meters (presbyopia)

Question 2

Explain why the sky appears blue and the sun appears reddish at sunrise or sunset.

Solution:

Blue Sky:

  • Sunlight contains seven colors
  • When sunlight travels through the atmosphere, it encounters air molecules
  • These molecules scatter different wavelengths of light differently
  • Blue light is scattered more than other colors (Rayleigh scattering)
  • This scattered blue light reaches our eyes from all directions
  • Hence, the sky appears blue

Red Sun at Sunrise/Sunset:

  • At sunrise/sunset, sunlight travels a longer path through the atmosphere
  • Most of the blue light gets scattered away
  • Red light, having longer wavelength, is scattered least
  • Only the red light reaches our eyes
  • Therefore, the sun appears reddish

Key Formulas

Power of a Lens

P = 1/f

Where:

  • P = Power in diopters (D)
  • f = Focal length in meters (m)

Lens Formula

1/f = 1/v - 1/u

Where:

  • f = Focal length
  • v = Image distance
  • u = Object distance

Magnification

m = -v/u

Where:

  • m = Magnification
  • v = Image distance
  • u = Object distance

Common Misconceptions

Color of Objects

Misconception: Objects have their own color.

Correction: Objects appear colored because they reflect certain wavelengths of light and absorb others. The color we see is the reflected light.

Rainbow Formation

Misconception: Rainbow is formed only due to refraction of light.

Correction: Rainbow formation involves both refraction and internal reflection of light in water droplets.

Vision Correction

Misconception: Wearing glasses makes your eyes dependent on them.

Correction: Glasses correct existing vision problems; they don't make eyes weaker or dependent.
