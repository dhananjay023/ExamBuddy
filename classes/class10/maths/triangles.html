Chapter 6: Triangles

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter builds upon your understanding of triangles from previous classes and introduces important concepts like similarity of triangles, theorems related to similar triangles, and their applications. The concepts learned here form the foundation for many advanced geometric concepts and have practical applications in real-world problem solving.

Key Concepts

Similar Triangles

  • Definition:
    • Two triangles are similar if their corresponding angles are equal
    • Corresponding sides are proportional
    • Symbol used: ~
  • Properties:
    • If two triangles are similar, their corresponding angles are equal
    • Ratio of corresponding sides is constant (similarity ratio)
    • Area ratio = (similarity ratio)²

Criteria for Similarity of Triangles

  • AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle) Criterion:
    • If corresponding angles of two triangles are equal
    • Third angle is automatically equal (sum of angles = 180°)
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side) Similarity:
    • Two sides proportional
    • Included angle equal
  • SSS (Side-Side-Side) Similarity:
    • All three sides proportional

Important Theorems

  • Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales Theorem):
    • Line parallel to one side of a triangle
    • Divides other two sides in the same ratio
  • Pythagoras Theorem:
    • In a right triangle
    • Square of hypotenuse = Sum of squares of other two sides
    • a² + b² = c², where c is hypotenuse
  • Angle Bisector Theorem:
    • Internal bisector of an angle of a triangle
    • Divides opposite side in ratio of lengths of other two sides

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

In triangle ABC, DE is parallel to BC and divides AB and AC in the ratio 2:3. If BC = 15 cm, find DE.

Solution:

Given: DE || BC and AB:AD = AC:AE = 2:3

  1. By Basic Proportionality Theorem:
    DE/BC = AD/AB = AE/AC = 2/5
  2. Given BC = 15 cm:
    DE/15 = 2/5
    DE = (2 × 15)/5
    DE = 6 cm

Therefore, DE = 6 cm

Question 2

In triangle ABC, ∠B = 90°. If AD ⊥ BC, prove that triangles ABC and ADB are similar.

Solution:

Given: ∠B = 90° and AD ⊥ BC

  1. In triangle ABC:
    • ∠B = 90° (given)
    • Let ∠BAC = x
    • ∠BCA = y
    • x + 90° + y = 180° (angle sum property)
  2. In triangle ADB:
    • ∠ADB = 90° (given, AD ⊥ BC)
    • ∠BAD = x (common angle)
    • ∠ABD = y (alternate angles, AD ⊥ BC)
  3. Therefore:
    • ∠BAC = ∠BAD (common)
    • ∠ABC = ∠ABD (both 90°)
    • ∠BCA = ∠BAD (proved)

By AAA similarity criterion, triangles ABC and ADB are similar.
