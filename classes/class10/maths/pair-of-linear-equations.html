Chapter 3: Pair of Linear Equations in Two Variables

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter deals with systems of two linear equations in two variables. You'll learn different methods to solve these equations and understand their graphical representation. The concepts learned here have wide applications in real-life situations involving two unknown quantities.

Key Concepts

Linear Equations

  • General form: ax + by + c = 0, where a, b are not both zero
  • A pair of linear equations:
    a₁x + b₁y + c₁ = 0
    a₂x + b₂y + c₂ = 0
  • Graphical representation is a straight line
  • Two equations together represent two lines

Types of Solutions

  • Unique Solution (Consistent):
    • Lines intersect at exactly one point
    • a₁/a₂ ≠ b₁/b₂
  • Infinitely Many Solutions (Dependent):
    • Lines are coincident
    • a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ = c₁/c₂
  • No Solution (Inconsistent):
    • Lines are parallel
    • a₁/a₂ = b₁/b₂ ≠ c₁/c₂

Methods of Solution

  • Substitution Method:
    • Express one variable in terms of other
    • Substitute in second equation
    • Solve for one variable
    • Find other variable
  • Elimination Method:
    • Multiply equations to make coefficients equal
    • Add or subtract equations
    • Solve for remaining variable
  • Cross Multiplication Method:
    • Useful when equations are in ratio form
    • Convert to standard form first

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Solve the following system of equations:

2x + 3y = 8
4x - 5y = 4

Solution:

Let's solve using elimination method:

  1. Multiply first equation by 2:
    4x + 6y = 16 ... (1)
    4x - 5y = 4 ... (2)
  2. Subtract equation (2) from (1):
    11y = 12
    y = 12/11
  3. Substitute y in equation (2):
    4x - 5(12/11) = 4
    4x = 4 + 60/11
    4x = 104/11
    x = 26/11

Therefore, x = 26/11 and y = 12/11

Question 2

The sum of numerator and denominator of a fraction is 12. If the numerator is increased by 2 and denominator is decreased by 1, the fraction becomes 1. Find the fraction.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Let numerator be x and denominator be y
    x + y = 12 ... (1)
  2. When numerator is increased by 2 and denominator is decreased by 1:
    (x + 2)/(y - 1) = 1
    x + 2 = y - 1 ... (2)
  3. From equation (2):
    y = x + 3 ... (3)
  4. Substitute (3) in (1):
    x + (x + 3) = 12
    2x + 3 = 12
    2x = 9
    x = 9/2
  5. Therefore:
    y = 9/2 + 3 = 15/2

The required fraction is 9/2 ÷ 15/2 = 9/15
