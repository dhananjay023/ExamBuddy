Chapter 8: Introduction to Trigonometry

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Trigonometry is the study of relationships between the sides and angles of triangles. This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of trigonometric ratios and their applications. Understanding these concepts is crucial for solving problems in mathematics, physics, engineering, and many other fields.

Key Concepts

Trigonometric Ratios

  • In a right triangle ABC, right-angled at B:
    sin A = Perpendicular/Hypotenuse = BC/AC
    cos A = Base/Hypotenuse = AB/AC
    tan A = Perpendicular/Base = BC/AB
  • Reciprocal ratios:
    cosec A = 1/sin A
    sec A = 1/cos A
    cot A = 1/tan A

Trigonometric Ratios of Standard Angles

Angle θ 0° 30° 45° 60° 90°
sin θ 0 1/2 1/√2 √3/2 1
cos θ 1 √3/2 1/√2 1/2 0
tan θ 0 1/√3 1 √3 ∞

Trigonometric Identities

  • Fundamental identities:
    sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
    1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
    1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
  • Quotient identities:
    tan θ = sin θ/cos θ
    cot θ = cos θ/sin θ

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Prove that (1 + tan²θ)(1 + cot²θ) = 4

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. We know that:
    • 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
    • 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ
  2. Therefore:
    • (1 + tan²θ)(1 + cot²θ) = sec²θ × cosec²θ
    • = (1/cos²θ) × (1/sin²θ)
    • = 1/(sin²θ × cos²θ)
    • = 1/[(sin θ × cos θ)²]
    • = 1/(1/4)² = 4

Hence proved that (1 + tan²θ)(1 + cot²θ) = 4

Question 2

If sin θ = 3/5, find the value of cos θ and tan θ.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Given: sin θ = 3/5
  2. Using sin²θ + cos²θ = 1:
    • (3/5)² + cos²θ = 1
    • 9/25 + cos²θ = 1
    • cos²θ = 1 - 9/25 = 16/25
    • cos θ = 4/5 (since θ is in first quadrant)
  3. Using tan θ = sin θ/cos θ:
    • tan θ = (3/5)/(4/5)
    • tan θ = 3/4

Therefore, cos θ = 4/5 and tan θ = 3/4

Key Points to Remember

Basic Ratios

sin θ = P/H
cos θ = B/H
tan θ = P/B

Reciprocal Relations

cosec θ = H/P = 1/sin θ
sec θ = H/B = 1/cos θ
cot θ = B/P = 1/tan θ
