Chapter 9: Some Applications of Trigonometry

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter explores practical applications of trigonometry in real-world scenarios. We'll learn how to calculate heights and distances of objects using angles of elevation and depression, making trigonometry a powerful tool for solving real-life problems in fields like engineering, architecture, and navigation.

Key Concepts

Angle of Elevation

  • Definition:

    The angle made by the line of sight with the horizontal when we look upward at an object

  • Key points:
    • Measured from the horizontal line
    • Always positive and less than 90°
    • Used when object is above eye level

Angle of Depression

  • Definition:

    The angle made by the line of sight with the horizontal when we look downward at an object

  • Key points:
    • Measured from the horizontal line
    • Equal to angle of elevation from the lower point
    • Used when object is below eye level

Height and Distance Problems

  • Common scenarios:
    • Height of buildings
    • Height of towers
    • Width of rivers
    • Distance between objects
  • Important considerations:
    • Line of sight
    • Horizontal distance
    • Observer's height
    • Base level differences

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

From a point on the ground, the angle of elevation of the top of a tower is 30°. On walking 20 meters towards the tower, the angle of elevation becomes 60°. Find the height of the tower.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Let the height of the tower be h meters
  2. Let the initial distance from the tower be x meters
  3. From first position:
    tan 30° = h/x
    (1/√3) = h/x
    h = x/√3
  4. From second position:
    tan 60° = h/(x-20)
    √3 = h/(x-20)
    h = √3(x-20)
  5. Equating both expressions:
    x/√3 = √3(x-20)
    x = 3(x-20)
    x = 3x-60
    -2x = -60
    x = 30
  6. Therefore:
    h = 30/√3 ≈ 17.32 meters

The height of the tower is approximately 17.32 meters.

Question 2

A man standing on the deck of a ship, which is 10 m above water level, observes the angle of depression of a boat as 45°. Calculate the distance of the boat from the ship.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Given:
    • Height above water level = 10 m
    • Angle of depression = 45°
  2. Using tan 45° = height/distance:
    1 = 10/x
    x = 10

The distance of the boat from the ship is 10 meters.

Problem-Solving Tips

Draw a Clear Diagram

  • Mark all given measurements
  • Label angles clearly
  • Show horizontal and vertical lines
  • Indicate observer's position

Choose the Right Ratio

  • Use sine for opposite/hypotenuse
  • Use cosine for adjacent/hypotenuse
  • Use tangent for opposite/adjacent
  • Consider the given angle carefully

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing elevation and depression angles
  • Forgetting observer's height
  • Using wrong trigonometric ratios
  • Not considering base level differences
