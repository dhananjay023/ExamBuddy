Chapter 10: Circles

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

A circle is a collection of points in a plane that are equidistant from a fixed point called the center. This chapter explores the properties of circles, including tangents, secants, and various theorems that help us understand the relationships between different parts of a circle.

Key Concepts

Basic Terms

  • Radius: Line segment from center to any point on circle
  • Diameter: Line segment through center connecting two points on circle
  • Chord: Line segment connecting any two points on circle
  • Arc: Part of circle between two points
  • Sector: Region bounded by two radii and an arc
  • Segment: Region bounded by a chord and an arc

Tangent Properties

  • Tangent-radius theorem:

    The tangent at any point of a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact

  • Length of tangents:

    Tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal in length

Circle Theorems

  • Angle in a semicircle:

    Angle in a semicircle is 90°

  • Equal chords:

    Equal chords are equidistant from the center

  • Perpendicular from center:

    The perpendicular from the center to a chord bisects the chord

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Two tangents TP and TQ are drawn to a circle with center O from an external point T. Prove that ∠PTQ = 2∠OTP.

Solution:

Let's prove this step by step:

  1. Given:
    • Circle with center O
    • T is an external point
    • TP and TQ are tangents
  2. Join OP and OQ:
    • OP ⊥ TP (radius ⊥ tangent)
    • OQ ⊥ TQ (radius ⊥ tangent)
    • OP = OQ (radii of same circle)
    • TP = TQ (tangents from external point)
  3. In triangles OTP and OTQ:
    • OP = OQ
    • TP = TQ
    • OT is common
    • Therefore, triangles are congruent
  4. Therefore:
    • ∠TOP = ∠TOQ
    • ∠PTQ = 2∠OTP

Hence proved that ∠PTQ = 2∠OTP

Question 2

If two circles intersect at points A and B, prove that their centers are equidistant from the common chord AB.

Solution:

Let's prove this step by step:

  1. Given:
    • Two circles with centers O₁ and O₂
    • Circles intersect at points A and B
  2. Draw perpendiculars O₁M and O₂N to AB:
    • O₁M ⊥ AB
    • O₂N ⊥ AB
  3. In right triangles O₁MA and O₂NA:
    • O₁A = O₁B (radii of first circle)
    • O₂A = O₂B (radii of second circle)
    • Therefore, M and N are midpoints of AB
    • Hence, M and N coincide
  4. Therefore:
    • O₁M = O₂N
    • The centers are equidistant from AB

Hence proved that the centers are equidistant from the common chord.

Important Theorems and Properties

Tangent-Radius Theorem

The tangent at any point of a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.

  • Helps in finding angle between tangent and radius
  • Used in construction of tangents
  • Important for proving other theorems

Equal Chords Theorem

Equal chords of a circle are equidistant from the center.

  • Helps in proving symmetry
  • Used in construction problems
  • Useful in finding distances

Angle in Semicircle

The angle in a semicircle is a right angle.

  • Used in construction of right angles
  • Helps in proving perpendicularity
  • Important in cyclic quadrilaterals
