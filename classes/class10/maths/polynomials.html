Chapter 2: Polynomials

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Polynomials is the second chapter in Class 10 Mathematics. This chapter builds on the concept of polynomials that you've studied in earlier classes and introduces new methods for finding zeros of polynomials, relationship between zeros and coefficients, and division algorithm for polynomials.

Key Concepts

Polynomials and Their Degrees

  • A polynomial p(x) in one variable x is an expression of the form p(x) = a₀ + a₁x + a₂x² + ... + aₙxⁿ, where a₀, a₁, a₂, ..., aₙ are constants and aₙ ≠ 0
  • The highest power of x in p(x) is called the degree of the polynomial
  • A polynomial of degree 1 is called a linear polynomial (e.g., 2x + 3)
  • A polynomial of degree 2 is called a quadratic polynomial (e.g., 3x² + 2x - 1)
  • A polynomial of degree 3 is called a cubic polynomial (e.g., x³ - 6x² + 11x - 6)

Zeros of a Polynomial

  • A real number k is called a zero of a polynomial p(x) if p(k) = 0
  • The zero of a polynomial is also called a root of the polynomial equation p(x) = 0
  • A polynomial of degree n has at most n zeros
  • Geometrically, zeros of a polynomial are the x-coordinates of the points where the graph of y = p(x) intersects the x-axis

Relationship Between Zeros and Coefficients

  • For a quadratic polynomial ax² + bx + c with zeros α and β:
    • Sum of zeros: α + β = -b/a
    • Product of zeros: α × β = c/a
  • For a cubic polynomial ax³ + bx² + cx + d with zeros α, β, and γ:
    • Sum of zeros: α + β + γ = -b/a
    • Sum of products of zeros taken two at a time: αβ + βγ + γα = c/a
    • Product of zeros: α × β × γ = -d/a

For Quadratic Polynomial ax² + bx + c with zeros α and β

α + β = -b/a
α × β = c/a

For Cubic Polynomial ax³ + bx² + cx + d with zeros α, β, and γ

α + β + γ = -b/a
αβ + βγ + γα = c/a
α × β × γ = -d/a

Division Algorithm for Polynomials

  • If p(x) and g(x) are two polynomials with g(x) ≠ 0, then there exist polynomials q(x) and r(x) such that:
    • p(x) = g(x) × q(x) + r(x)
    • where r(x) = 0 or degree of r(x) < degree of g(x)
  • q(x) is called the quotient and r(x) is called the remainder
  • When we divide p(x) by (x - a), the remainder is p(a)
  • This result is known as the Remainder Theorem
  • If p(a) = 0, then (x - a) is a factor of p(x)
  • This result is known as the Factor Theorem

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Find the zeros of the quadratic polynomial x² - 3x - 4 and verify the relationship between the zeros and the coefficients.

Solution:

Given polynomial: p(x) = x² - 3x - 4

Comparing with ax² + bx + c, we have a = 1, b = -3, c = -4

To find the zeros, we need to solve p(x) = 0:

x² - 3x - 4 = 0

Using the quadratic formula: x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)]/2a

x = [3 ± √(9 + 16)]/2 = [3 ± √25]/2 = [3 ± 5]/2

x = (3 + 5)/2 = 4 or x = (3 - 5)/2 = -1

So the zeros of the polynomial are α = 4 and β = -1.

Verification of relationship between zeros and coefficients:

Sum of zeros: α + β = 4 + (-1) = 3

According to the formula: α + β = -b/a = -(-3)/1 = 3

Product of zeros: α × β = 4 × (-1) = -4

According to the formula: α × β = c/a = -4/1 = -4

Hence, the relationship between the zeros and coefficients is verified.

Question 2

Use the remainder theorem to find the remainder when p(x) = x³ - 4x² + 5x - 3 is divided by (x - 2).

Solution:

Given polynomial: p(x) = x³ - 4x² + 5x - 3

According to the remainder theorem, when p(x) is divided by (x - a), the remainder is p(a).

Here, we need to find the remainder when p(x) is divided by (x - 2), so we need to find p(2).

p(2) = (2)³ - 4(2)² + 5(2) - 3

p(2) = 8 - 16 + 10 - 3

p(2) = -1

Therefore, the remainder when p(x) is divided by (x - 2) is -1.

Question 3

If α and β are the zeros of the polynomial 2x² - 5x + 7, find the value of:

(i) α + β

(ii) α × β

(iii) α² + β²

(iv) 1/α + 1/β

Solution:

Given polynomial: p(x) = 2x² - 5x + 7

Comparing with ax² + bx + c, we have a = 2, b = -5, c = 7

(i) α + β

Using the relation: α + β = -b/a

α + β = -(-5)/2 = 5/2

(ii) α × β

Using the relation: α × β = c/a

α × β = 7/2

(iii) α² + β²

We know that: α² + β² = (α + β)² - 2(α × β)

α² + β² = (5/2)² - 2(7/2)

α² + β² = 25/4 - 7

α² + β² = 25/4 - 28/4

α² + β² = -3/4

(iv) 1/α + 1/β

We know that: 1/α + 1/β = (α + β)/(α × β)

1/α + 1/β = (5/2)/(7/2)

1/α + 1/β = 5/7

Key Formulas

Remainder Theorem

If p(x) is divided by (x - a), remainder = p(a)

Factor Theorem

(x - a) is a factor of p(x) if and only if p(a) = 0

Quadratic Polynomial Zeros

α + β = -b/a, α × β = c/a

Cubic Polynomial Zeros

α + β + γ = -b/a, αβ + βγ + γα = c/a, αβγ = -d/a
