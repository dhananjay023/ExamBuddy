Chapter 4: Quadratic Equations

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

A quadratic equation is a polynomial equation of degree 2. This chapter explores methods to solve quadratic equations, understand the nature of their roots, and apply these concepts to solve real-world problems. The skills learned here are fundamental to higher mathematics and have practical applications in physics, engineering, and economics.

Key Concepts

Standard Form

  • A quadratic equation in variable x is of the form:
    ax² + bx + c = 0
    where:
    • a ≠ 0
    • a, b, c are real numbers
    • a is the coefficient of x²
    • b is the coefficient of x
    • c is the constant term

Methods of Solution

  • Factorization Method:
    • Split the middle term
    • Find factors that add to give b and multiply to give ac
    • Write as product of linear factors
  • Completing the Square:
    • Rearrange to group x terms
    • Add square of half coefficient of x
    • Write as perfect square trinomial
  • Quadratic Formula:
    x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)]/2a

Nature of Roots

  • Discriminant (D) = b² - 4ac
  • If D > 0:
    • Two distinct real roots
    • Roots are real and unequal
  • If D = 0:
    • Two equal real roots
    • Roots are real and equal
  • If D < 0:
    • No real roots
    • Roots are complex conjugates

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Solve the quadratic equation: x² - 5x + 6 = 0 using factorization method.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Identify a = 1, b = -5, c = 6
  2. Find factors of 6 that add up to -5:
    • Factors of 6: ±1, ±2, ±3, ±6
    • -2 and -3 add up to -5 and multiply to give 6
  3. Write equation as product of factors:
    x² - 5x + 6 = 0
    (x - 2)(x - 3) = 0
  4. Using null factor property:
    x - 2 = 0 or x - 3 = 0
    x = 2 or x = 3

Therefore, the roots are x = 2 and x = 3

Question 2

Find the nature of roots of the quadratic equation: 2x² + x + 1 = 0

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Identify coefficients:
    • a = 2
    • b = 1
    • c = 1
  2. Calculate discriminant (D):
    D = b² - 4ac
    D = (1)² - 4(2)(1)
    D = 1 - 8
    D = -7
  3. Since D < 0:
    • The equation has no real roots
    • The roots are complex conjugates

Therefore, the equation has no real roots
