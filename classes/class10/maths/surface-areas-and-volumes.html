Chapter 12: Surface Areas and Volumes

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter explores the calculation of surface areas and volumes of three-dimensional shapes. Understanding these concepts is crucial for real-world applications in architecture, engineering, and design. We'll learn how to calculate the amount of material needed to cover 3D objects and the space they occupy.

Key Concepts

Cube and Cuboid (3D)

  • Cube (3D):
    Surface Area = 6a²
    Volume = a³
    where a is the edge length
  • Cuboid (3D):
    Surface Area = 2(lb + bh + hl)
    Volume = l × b × h
    where l, b, h are length, breadth, height

Cylinder and Cone (3D)

  • Cylinder (3D):
    Curved Surface Area = 2πrh
    Total Surface Area = 2πr(h + r)
    Volume = πr²h
    where r is radius, h is height
  • Cone (3D):
    Curved Surface Area = πrl
    Total Surface Area = πr(l + r)
    Volume = ⅓πr²h
    where l is slant height

Sphere and Hemisphere (3D)

  • Sphere (3D):
    Surface Area = 4πr²
    Volume = (4/3)πr³
  • Hemisphere (3D):
    Curved Surface Area = 2πr²
    Total Surface Area = 3πr²
    Volume = (2/3)πr³

Related Shapes and Transformations

Cube Related Shapes

  • 2D Shapes:
    • Square (faces)

    • Rectangle (edge view)
  • 3D Variations:
    • Rectangular prism (stretched cube)
    • Truncated cube
    • Cube with cylindrical hole

Cylinder Related Shapes

  • 2D Shapes:
    • Circle (base and top)
    • Rectangle (lateral surface when unrolled)
    • Ellipse (when viewed at an angle)
  • 3D Variations:
    • Hollow cylinder
    • Truncated cylinder
    • Oblique cylinder

Cone Related Shapes

  • 2D Shapes:
    • Circle (base)
    • Sector (lateral surface when unrolled)
    • Triangle (cross-section)
  • 3D Variations:
    • Truncated cone
    • Double cone
    • Oblique cone

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

A cylindrical water tank has a radius of 2.1 meters and height of 3.5 meters. Find:

  1. The amount of metal sheet needed to make the tank (total surface area)
  2. The capacity of the tank in liters

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Total Surface Area:
    TSA = 2πr(h + r)
    = 2π(2.1)(3.5 + 2.1)
    = 2π(2.1)(5.6)
    = 73.82 m²
  2. Volume in liters:
    V = πr²h
    = π(2.1)²(3.5)
    = 48.45 m³
    = 48,450 liters
    (1 m³ = 1000 liters)

Therefore, 73.82 m² of metal sheet is needed, and the tank can hold 48,450 liters of water.

Question 2

A cone has a radius of 5 cm and a slant height of 13 cm. Find its height and volume.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Finding height using Pythagorean theorem:
    l² = r² + h²
    13² = 5² + h²
    169 = 25 + h²
    h² = 144
    h = 12 cm
  2. Calculating volume:
    V = ⅓πr²h
    = ⅓π(5)²(12)
    = ⅓π(25)(12)
    = 314.16 cm³

Therefore, the height of the cone is 12 cm and its volume is approximately 314.16 cubic centimeters.
