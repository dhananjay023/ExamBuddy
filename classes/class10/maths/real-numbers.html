Chapter 1: Real Numbers

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

Real Numbers is the first chapter in Class 10 Mathematics. It builds upon your understanding of number systems from previous classes and introduces important concepts like Euclid's Division Lemma, the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, and more. This chapter provides the foundation for various mathematical operations and proofs used throughout your mathematical journey.

Key Concepts

1. Euclid's Division Lemma

For any positive integers a and b, there exist unique integers q (quotient) and r (remainder) that satisfy:

a = bq + r, where 0 ≤ r < b

Example:

If we divide 17 by 3:

  • 17 = 3 × 5 + 2
  • Here, a = 17, b = 3, q = 5, r = 2

2. The Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic

Every composite number can be expressed as a unique product of prime numbers (up to the order of their appearance).

Example:

Let's find prime factorization of 84:

  • 84 = 2 × 42
  • 42 = 2 × 21
  • 21 = 3 × 7
  • Therefore, 84 = 2² × 3 × 7

3. HCF and LCM

For two positive integers a and b:

HCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b) = a × b

Example:

Find HCF and LCM of 12 and 18:

  • 12 = 2² × 3
  • 18 = 2 × 3²
  • HCF = 2 × 3 = 6
  • LCM = 2² × 3² = 36
  • Verify: 6 × 36 = 12 × 18 = 216

4. Irrational Numbers

Numbers that cannot be expressed as p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.

  • Examples: √2, √3, π, e
  • Every real number is either rational or irrational
  • The sum and product of a rational and an irrational number is irrational

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

Use Euclid's division algorithm to find the HCF of 135 and 225.

Solution:

Using Euclid's algorithm:

  1. 225 = 135 × 1 + 90
  2. 135 = 90 × 1 + 45
  3. 90 = 45 × 2 + 0

Therefore, HCF(135, 225) = 45

Question 2

Show that √3 is irrational.

Solution:

Let's use proof by contradiction:

  1. Assume √3 is rational
  2. Then √3 = p/q where p, q are coprime integers
  3. Therefore, 3 = p²/q²
  4. 3q² = p²
  5. This means p² is divisible by 3
  6. Therefore p is divisible by 3
  7. Let p = 3k
  8. Then 3q² = 9k²
  9. Therefore q² = 3k²
  10. This means q is also divisible by 3
  11. This contradicts that p and q are coprime

Therefore, √3 is irrational.
