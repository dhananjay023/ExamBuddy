Chapter 11: Areas Related to Circles

Class 10 Mathematics Updated: May 15, 2025

Introduction

This chapter focuses on calculating areas of various parts of a circle and regions related to circles. We'll learn how to find areas of sectors, segments, and combinations of circular regions, which have practical applications in engineering, architecture, and everyday life.

Key Concepts

Area Formulas

  • Circle:
    Area = πr²
    where r is the radius
  • Sector:
    Area = (θ/360°) × πr²
    where θ is the angle in degrees
  • Segment:
    Area = Area of sector - Area of triangle
    = (θ/360°)πr² - ½r²sin θ

Length Formulas

  • Circumference:
    C = 2πr
  • Arc length:
    L = (θ/360°) × 2πr
    where θ is the angle in degrees
  • Perimeter of sector:
    P = 2r + L
    where L is the arc length

Special Cases

  • Semicircle:
    Area = ½πr²
    Perimeter = πr + 2r
  • Quarter circle:
    Area = ¼πr²
    Perimeter = (π/2)r + 2r
  • Annular region:
    Area = π(R² - r²)
    where R and r are outer and inner radii

Important Questions and Solutions

Question 1

A circular park has a circumference of 176 meters. Eight identical flower beds are laid along its boundary. Find the area of each flower bed if they cover 35% of the park's area.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Find the radius of the park:
    2πr = 176
    r = 28 meters
  2. Calculate total area of the park:
    A = πr²
    A = π(28)²
    A = 2,464 sq meters
  3. Calculate total area of flower beds:
    35% of 2,464 = 862.4 sq meters
  4. Area of each flower bed:
    862.4 ÷ 8 = 107.8 sq meters

Therefore, each flower bed has an area of 107.8 square meters.

Question 2

Find the area of the shaded region in a circle of radius 14 cm if the angle of the sector is 60°.

Solution:

Let's solve this step by step:

  1. Area of sector:
    = (60/360) × πr²
    = (1/6) × π(14)²
    = (1/6) × 616π
    = 102.67π sq cm
  2. Area of triangle:
    = ½ × r² × sin 60°
    = ½ × 196 × (√3/2)
    = 84.87 sq cm
  3. Area of segment:
    = Area of sector - Area of triangle
    = 102.67π - 84.87
    ≈ 237.93 sq cm

Therefore, the area of the shaded region is approximately 237.93 square centimeters.

Problem-Solving Tips

Drawing Clear Diagrams

  • Label all dimensions clearly
  • Mark angles and arcs
  • Shade regions when needed
  • Show all relevant measurements

Choosing the Right Formula

  • Identify the type of region
  • Check if it's a combination of regions
  • Consider special cases
  • Verify units are consistent

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using diameter instead of radius
  • Forgetting to convert degrees
  • Mixing up sector and segment
  • Incorrect unit conversions
