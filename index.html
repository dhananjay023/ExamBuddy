Class 10 Science: Electricity

Chapter 12

Key Concepts

  • Electric current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor
  • The SI unit of electric current is ampere (A)
  • The potential difference between two points is the work done to move a unit charge from one point to another
  • Ohm's law states that the current through a conductor is proportional to the voltage across it (V = IR)
  • The SI unit of resistance is ohm (Î©)

Important Questions

Q1: State Ohm's law. How is it verified experimentally?

Solution: Ohm's law states that the current flowing through a conductor is directly proportional to the potential difference applied across its ends, provided the temperature and other physical conditions remain constant.

Mathematically, V = IR, where V is the potential difference, I is the current, and R is the resistance.

Experimental verification:

  • Set up a circuit with a battery, ammeter, voltmeter, resistor, and rheostat
  • Vary the resistance using the rheostat
  • Record the readings of current and voltage for different resistance values
  • Plot a graph of V vs I - a straight line indicates Ohm's law is verified
